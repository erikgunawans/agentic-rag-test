2026 Design Refresh — Direction

Your current design already has strong 2026 foundations (OKLCH, glassmorphism, Geist, dot-grid). Here are two approaches for refinement.

A

Calibrated Restraint (Recommended)

The Linear/Vercel aesthetic. Shift base color from navy-blue (#0B1120) to zinc-neutral (#09090B). Reduce border opacity from 8% to 5%. Remove glass from persistent panels (sidebars) — keep it only for transient overlays (tooltips, popovers). Flatten the gradient send button to solid fill. Tighten heading letter-spacing. Snappier transitions.


What changes: Color palette (zinc base), border subtlety, sidebar treatment, button style, typography tracking, transition timing
What stays: Dot-grid, mesh-bg, floating orbs, Geist font, overall layout structure
Risk: Low — CSS-only changes, no component restructuring

B

Enhanced Current (Preserve Navy Identity)

Keep the navy-blue personality. Stay with #0B1120 base but refine the supporting palette. Reduce border opacity slightly (8% → 6%). Keep glass sidebars but make them subtler (lower blur, higher transparency). Tighten typography. Add snappier easing curves. Keep gradient buttons but refine the gradient to be less saturated.


What changes: Border subtlety, glass refinement, typography, transitions, gradient polish
What stays: Navy color identity, glass sidebars, gradient buttons, all current effects
Risk: Minimal — lighter touch, fewer changes
